# GSC & GA4 Organic Search Audit — Stolen Goat

**Date:** 2026-03-24
**Period:** 2025-12-22 to 2026-03-21 (90 days)
**Sources:** Google Search Console, GA4 (properties/310109076, China bot traffic excluded)

---

## Executive Summary

Stolen Goat's organic search is heavily brand-dependent. Branded queries account for 62% of clicks from the top 200 queries despite only 12.5% of those queries being branded. Non-branded organic visibility is dominated by a single blog post (cycling events 2026) and generic product category pages that sit mostly on page 2+. There are 1,031 striking-distance keywords (position 5-20) representing a substantial untapped opportunity — particularly in high-volume categories like cycling jerseys, socks, gilets, and arm warmers.

Organic traffic delivered 12,783 sessions, 153 purchases, and GBP 11,181 revenue over 90 days (1.20% conversion rate). The top revenue-generating organic landing page is the homepage, followed by the Climb & Conquer gloves product page.

## Key Findings & Recommendations for Strategy

### Strengths
1. Strong brand search presence — position 1-2 for core branded terms
2. Blog content driving significant organic impressions (cycling events, running, thermolite)
3. Active site search with clear demand signals
4. Reasonable 1.20% organic conversion rate

### Weaknesses
1. **Over-reliance on branded traffic** — 62% of clicks from branded queries
2. **Category pages rank poorly** — most product category pages sit on page 2+ (position 10-15)
3. **Massive wasted impressions** — 59,120 impressions for mens jerseys category but only 600 clicks (1.0% CTR)
4. **Content pages don't convert** — top blog posts drive zero purchases
5. **Key product categories underperform** — cycling socks (pos 10.7), cycling jersey (pos 12.2), bib shorts (pos 24.5)

### Priority Opportunities
1. **Category page optimisation** — improving positions for cycling jerseys, socks, gilets, and arm warmers could unlock thousands of incremental clicks
2. **Content-to-commerce bridging** — blog posts need better CTAs/internal linking to product pages
3. **Running content** — 59,566 impressions for "how to start running for beginners" at position 8.1 — optimise for a quick win, but assess commercial relevance
4. **1,031 striking distance keywords** — systematic on-page optimisation programme for position 5-20 keywords
5. **Blog content strategy** — the cycling events 2026 post proves content can drive traffic; replicate this model for other seasonal/evergreen topics
