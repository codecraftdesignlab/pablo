## 5. Summary of Findings

### SG's Current Strengths
- Jerseys: #4-16 for "cycle jersey/cycling jersey" variants
- Tri suits: #9-20 for tri suit terms (direct overlap with Castelli)
- Arm warmers: #2
- Cycling socks: #6
- Leg warmers: #10 (for "legging warmers" variant)
- Cycling gilet: #9 in SERP

### Critical Gaps
1. **Women's cycling shorts** — The most valuable gap by far. SG has zero visibility across a keyword cluster worth 250,000+ monthly searches where Le Col and Rapha dominate.
2. **General cycling clothing terms** — SG is invisible for "cycling clothing", "cycling apparel", and "bike outfits" head terms (combined ~60,000 monthly searches).
3. **Men's cycling shorts** — SG ranks poorly (#90-96) despite having product. Competitors hold top-10 positions.
4. **Cycling gloves** — SG ranks #82 despite having a gloves category. Castelli ranks #9.

### Strategic Implications
- **Le Col's playbook** is collection-page SEO. Their `/collections/womens-bib-shorts` page captures 20+ keyword variants. SG should replicate this approach.
- **Rapha's breadth** comes from carrying more product categories (helmets, shoes, cargo). SG can compete on the clothing categories they share.
- **Castelli's strength** in men's shorts and gloves is category-page driven — same approach SG should adopt.
- **Editorial content** (buying guides, "best X" articles) is largely absent from all four competitors' strategies. This is an opportunity for SG to differentiate, particularly for gift/seasonal terms and "best cycling jersey UK"-type queries where review sites currently dominate.
