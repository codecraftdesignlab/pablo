## 3. Keyword Gap Analysis

The formal gap analysis (SG vs santinisms.com + kalas.cz + lecol.cc) returned 100 gap keywords. However, because santinisms.com and kalas.cz (international) have minimal UK data, the results are dominated by Le Col's lifestyle keywords.

**CK-relevant gaps from the formal analysis:** None directly — the gap was all lifestyle terms (shorts, jerseys, base layers).

**The real CK gap is better identified by SERP analysis:** SG does not appear in the top 20 for ANY of the 8 primary CK search terms tested. This is not a "gap" in the traditional sense (competitor ranks, we don't) — it is a complete absence. SG has no CK-targeted landing pages ranking organically.

## 6. Key Findings Summary

1. **SG has zero organic visibility for CK keywords.** Not a single CK-relevant term ranks in the top 20. This is the biggest gap identified.

2. **The CK SERP is dominated by specialists**, not big brands. owayo, Velotec, Spized, Gobik, and Raceskin are niche players who rank through dedicated, well-optimised CK pages. SG can absolutely compete here.

3. **Kalas (kalas.co.uk) is the strongest CK competitor in organic search** among the three named competitors. They rank top-10 for 10+ CK terms. Le Col and Santini are not organic CK competitors.

4. **The winning page type is a dedicated CK landing page** — not a blog post, not a product page. It needs process info, visuals, and strong CTAs.

5. **Seasonality is pronounced.** CK keyword volumes peak March–June, with a secondary spike in September. Content should be published and indexed by February at the latest.

6. **High CPC (£2–5+) confirms strong commercial value.** These are high-intent, purchase-ready searches.

7. **"No minimum order" is a differentiator** appearing in SERPs — if SG offers low/no MOQs, this should be prominent.

8. **The CK market extends beyond cycling** — triathlon suits (210/mo), running vests (320/mo), and MTB jerseys (170/mo) are meaningful adjacent opportunities.
